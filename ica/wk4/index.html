Identity & Culture: How do your color and typography choices reflect your personal or cultural identity? Every choice you make reflects your personal or cultural identity, in my case, I felt like using the stupidest font color combination I could muster. First I tried to use papyrun but it wasnt on google fonts so I was relegated to comic sans What visual elements feel most authentic to you and why? The font I chose felt very authentic to me because that is the kind of thing I like to do on assignments like this and I felt no desire to comprimise. Accessibility Considerations: I made sure the text I chose was going to be as legible as possible with as much contrast as feasible.

How did accessibility requirements influence your design decisions? The accessibility requirements forced me to keep the text legible, secretly I wanted to choose a way more ridiculous font that would have been alot harder to read. What challenges did you face balancing personal expression with accessibility? I had to comprimise somehwhat on the exact font partictularly because I couldnt be bothered to get my dad to send me the verification code I need anytime I sign into CC on the web. Design Process: I picked a font and colors then followed the instructions

What surprised you about creating a cohesive design system? I didnt know how to use margins like this until today. How will you apply this system to your portfolio project? I will be using the margin system and the form input possibly

How might you expand or refine this system for different contexts? If I wanted to write up a technical page for my data sci work I would probably make a code window with a white text on black style and make sure the contrast is good before releasing it. What aspects of your system feel most/least flexible? The buttons at the end which I though were super cool.
